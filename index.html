Internships
I worked with the Intelligent Robotics Group at NASA Ames on the free-flying robot,Astrobee. I developed a bootloader for the dsPIC33EP512MC806 microcontroller by Microchip that is to be used on the perching arm subsystem of the robot. I also designed and implemented a variety of reliability tests for the propulsion and perching arm systems. In addition to those tests, I also conducted a series of tests on LEDs to determine the viability of implementing a visual signaling system on the robot.
I assisted two undergraduate students with their surgery related research projects. One of the studies examined the safety of a gastric bypass after a sleeve gastroplasty. The other study compared various laparoscopic vessel sealing energy devices. For my help in their research I was named one of the main authors in both studies, which were published in the Society of American Gastrointestinal and Endoscopic Surgeons(SAGES) journal.
I developed software utilized in an embedded encoder monitoring system that employed cloud computing for data analysis and data visualization. I designed and conducted thermal and battery tests for the prototype encoder system. I also began development on a mesh network system to help with implementation of the new encoders in extreme industrial settings. I also designed tools and fixtures that facilitated encoder construction. I used 3D printers on a daily basis to rapidly iterate through designs.
